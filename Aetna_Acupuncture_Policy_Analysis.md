## 1. Policy Overview

### Scope
- Clinical Policy Bulletin addressing acupuncture and dry needling
- Applies to standard Aetna plans
- Coverage extended when administered by health care provider practicing within scope of license
- Some plans limit coverage to use in lieu of other anesthesia for surgical/dental procedures

### Coverage Model
- **Most Restrictive** of major payers
- Only 8 specific conditions covered as medically necessary
- **118 conditions explicitly listed as experimental/investigational/unproven**
- Dry needling NOT covered (experimental)
- Maintenance treatment explicitly NOT covered
- 4-week reassessment requirement

## 2. Covered Conditions (Medically Necessary)

### Approved Indications for Acupuncture (Manual or Electroacupuncture)

| # | Condition | Special Requirements |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Chronic Neck Pain | **Minimum 12 weeks duration required** |
| 2 | Chronic Headache | **Minimum 12 weeks duration required** |
| 3 | Low Back Pain | No duration requirement |
| 4 | Nausea of Pregnancy | None |
| 5 | Osteoarthritis Pain (Knee or Hip) | **Adjunctive therapy only** |
| 6 | Post-operative and Chemotherapy-induced Nausea/Vomiting | None |
| 7 | Post-operative Dental Pain | None |
| 8 | Temporomandibular Disorders (TMD) | None |

### Critical Coverage Limitations

| Limitation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Chronicity Requirement** | Neck pain and headache must be chronic (≥12 weeks) |
| **Adjunctive Therapy** | OA pain coverage is only as adjunct to other treatment |
| **4-Week Reassessment** | If no clinical benefit after 4 weeks, treatment plan must be reevaluated |
| **Meaningful Improvement** | Further treatment not covered if no meaningful improvement demonstrated |
| **Maintenance Exclusion** | Treatment where symptoms neither regressing nor improving = NOT covered |

## 4. Claim Denial Rules

### DR-001: Non-Covered Diagnosis (Experimental/Investigational)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Rule ID** | DR-001 |
| **Rule Name** | Experimental/Investigational Condition |
| **Condition** | Diagnosis code matches one of 118 excluded conditions |
| **Action** | DENY entire claim |
| **Denial Reason** | Acupuncture for this indication is experimental, investigational, or unproven |

**Key Excluded Diagnoses:**
- All infertility codes
- All fibromyalgia codes
- All anxiety/depression codes
- All addiction codes
- Carpal tunnel syndrome
- Plantar fasciitis
- Tennis elbow
- Whiplash
- Any condition not in the 8 approved list

---

### DR-002: Chronicity Requirement Not Met

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Rule ID** | DR-002 |
| **Rule Name** | Chronicity Requirement |
| **Condition** | Neck pain or headache diagnosis without 12-week duration documented |
| **Applicable Diagnoses** | Chronic neck pain, Chronic headache |
| **Documentation Required** | Symptom onset date demonstrating ≥12 weeks duration |
| **Action** | DENY claim |
| **Denial Reason** | Condition must be chronic (minimum 12 weeks duration) for coverage |

**Example:**
- Neck pain onset: November 1, 2025
- Treatment date: November 15, 2025
- Duration: 2 weeks
- **Result**: DENY - Does not meet 12-week chronicity requirement

## 5. Exploitation Tactics and Detection Patterns

### ET-001: Diagnosis Code Manipulation

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-001 |
| **Tactic Name** | Diagnosis Code Manipulation |
| **Method** | Provider assigns covered diagnosis (e.g., chronic neck pain M54.2) when treating experimental condition (e.g., fibromyalgia, carpal tunnel) |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Upcoding to avoid 118-condition exclusion list |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Diagnosis inconsistency | Notes describe excluded condition |
| Treatment points | Acupoints inconsistent with billed diagnosis |
| Prior claims | History of claims for excluded conditions |
| Referral source | Referral for excluded condition |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- Provider with high volume of "chronic neck pain" or "low back pain" diagnoses
- Documentation mentions fibromyalgia, carpal tunnel, or other excluded conditions
- Patient history shows prior diagnoses for excluded conditions

---

### ET-002: Chronicity Fabrication

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-002 |
| **Tactic Name** | Chronicity Fabrication |
| **Method** | Provider documents 12+ weeks duration for neck pain/headache without actual chronic history |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Fabricating chronicity to meet coverage requirement |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Prior claims | No prior medical claims for condition in 12 weeks |
| Documentation | Vague onset date or "patient reports chronic" |
| New patient | First visit with chronic diagnosis |
| Cross-reference | No PCP or specialist visits for condition |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- New patient presenting with "12+ weeks" chronic condition
- No supporting claims history for chronic condition
- Documentation lacks specific onset date

---

### ET-003: Perpetual Treatment Without Improvement

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-003 |
| **Tactic Name** | Perpetual Treatment |
| **Method** | Provider continues treatment indefinitely by documenting minimal "improvement" to avoid maintenance classification |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Circumventing 4-week reassessment and meaningful improvement requirements |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Treatment duration | > 8 weeks without significant documented improvement |
| Visit pattern | Regular visits (weekly/bi-weekly) ongoing |
| Documentation | Same findings visit after visit |
| Outcome measures | Minimal or no change in pain/function scores |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- Patient receiving treatment > 12 weeks same condition
- No objective improvement documented
- Same treatment plan throughout

---

### ET-004: Adjunctive Therapy Circumvention

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-004 |
| **Tactic Name** | Adjunctive Therapy Circumvention |
| **Method** | Provider bills OA acupuncture without actual adjunctive treatment, or creates fictitious PT/medication history |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Claiming adjunctive status without primary therapy |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Concurrent claims | No PT, medication, or injection claims |
| Documentation | Vague reference to "other treatment" |
| Referral | No orthopedic or rheumatology referral |
| Medication history | No NSAIDs or OA medications in pharmacy claims |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- OA diagnosis with acupuncture but no other OA treatment claims
- No concurrent PT claims within 90 days
- No relevant medications in pharmacy profile

---

### ET-005: Dry Needling as Acupuncture

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-005 |
| **Tactic Name** | Dry Needling Disguised as Acupuncture |
| **Method** | Provider performs dry needling but bills as acupuncture to obtain coverage |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Billing experimental procedure as covered service |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Provider type | Physical therapist billing acupuncture codes |
| Documentation | Notes describe "trigger points," "myofascial," "dry needling" |
| Diagnosis | Myofascial pain (excluded condition) |
| Treatment description | Focus on muscle trigger points vs. meridian points |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- Non-acupuncturist billing acupuncture codes
- Documentation uses dry needling terminology
- Diagnosis is myofascial pain or trigger point-related

---

### ET-006: Diagnosis Cycling

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-006 |
| **Tactic Name** | Diagnosis Cycling |
| **Method** | When one covered diagnosis reaches 4-week no-benefit threshold, provider switches to different covered diagnosis |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Rotating through 8 covered diagnoses to extend treatment |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Diagnosis changes | New covered diagnosis every 4-6 weeks |
| Sequential pattern | One diagnosis ends, another begins immediately |
| Coverage cycle | Rotating through neck → back → headache → TMD |
| Documentation | New complaint appears when prior plateaus |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- Patient with 3+ different covered diagnoses in 6 months
- Each diagnosis treated 4-6 weeks then switched
- No gap between diagnosis episodes

---

### ET-007: Post-Dating Chronicity

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-007 |
| **Tactic Name** | Post-Dating Chronicity |
| **Method** | Provider documents onset date 12+ weeks prior even though patient is new or condition is recent |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Backdating symptom onset to meet chronicity requirement |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| New patient | Chronic diagnosis on first visit |
| Onset date | Exactly 12 weeks or 3 months prior |
| No corroboration | No prior medical records supporting onset |
| Round numbers | Onset dates on 1st of month or round timeframes |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- Onset date suspiciously exactly 12 weeks before treatment
- No corroborating medical records
- Pattern of all patients having 12-week onset dates

---

### ET-008: Minimal Improvement Documentation

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Tactic ID** | ET-008 |
| **Tactic Name** | Minimal Improvement Gaming |
| **Method** | Provider documents just enough "improvement" to avoid 4-week reassessment denial, without actual clinical change |
| **Abuse Pattern** | Documenting false incremental improvement |

**Detection Indicators:**
| Indicator | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Improvement pattern | Same minimal improvement every 4 weeks |
| Documentation | Generic language like "patient reports slight improvement" |
| Objective measures | VAS drops from 7 to 6 repeatedly |
| Treatment unchanged | Same protocol despite "improvement" |

**Audit Flag Criteria:**
- Documented improvement exactly at 4-week intervals
- No change in treatment despite documented improvement
- Pattern of minimal improvement across all patients
